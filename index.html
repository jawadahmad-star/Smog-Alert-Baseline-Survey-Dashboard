Survey at a Glance

Real-time monitoring of the Smog Alert Baseline Survey in Shalamar Tehsil, Lahore. The study measures household experiences with air quality and smog — health impacts, beliefs about pollution sources, information channels, take-up of the Smog Alert mobile app, and willingness to pay for cleaner air — tracked against a 300-household sampling frame across 13 union councils, with a 3-arm randomized information experiment embedded in the interview.

Beliefs & the Information Experiment

The heart of the study. Respondents allocate 100 tokens across pollution sources before and after a randomized information script: T1 hears a Lahore-specific report (vehicles ≈ 83/100), T2 hears a Punjab-wide report (vehicles ≈ 43/100), and Control hears nothing. Downstream, everyone plays a real-stakes donation game and can sign an enforcement letter to the Urban Unit.

Willingness to Pay & Fairness

Would households contribute to cleaner air? A 2×2 scenario-based contingent-valuation design crosses source (vehicles vs crop burning) with policy type (monitoring vs enforcement), with the monthly bid randomized at PKR 100 / 200 / 500 per household — plus fairness views and the green-sticker vehicle-compliance module.
